Harness That Imagination With Coding!

Overview

People honestly don't give children enough credit, if properly instructed in an engaging way they are fast learners.

That's because the imagination they have for the world around them is overwhelmingly full of wonder and awe.

In this day and age of digital wonder teaching methods are increasingly utilizing technology to make learning more efficient and fun for children.
So why not give students in elementary schools the ability to have fun with their imagination while learning and implement more coding into their
education?

Main reasons why early ciriculum should incorporate learning to code:

  1. Learning to code at a young age gives a competitive advantage later on in life
  2. Programmers are in high demand
  3. Coding teaches kids useful problem-solving skills
  4. Helps strengthen and improve creativity at an early age
  5. Helps to build communication and collaboration skills

Ways to engage and stimulate interest in coding at a young age:

There are many activities that children take part in that can peek their interest into wanting to learn how to code

  1. What kid doesn't love video games?

    • Most kids are constantly picking up video games to play and enjoy with their friends, and in my personal experience
      at that age just playing video games makes you want to be able to create your own game where you can dream up a world to play in.

    • There are even some video games these days that will teach kids basic coding skills, one example would be the popular game: "MineCraft"

  2. Legos: Don't just build your imagination, code it!

    • legos have been a staple in childhood around the world, but you can do more than just build with legos, you can use them to build robots
      and upload code to give them a brain!

    • Lego Robot competitions are already established events around the world, these competitions teach kids adversity, creativity, and problem solving
      in fun and engaging ways. These also provide oppertunities for parents to help their kid and bond with doing something they enjoy!

  3. Website building!
    • Growing up with the internet you end up seeing tons of interesting websites to explore or play on, having access to all the unique
      environments often drives a child's desire to want to create their own, that was the case for some of my friends and I growing up.
